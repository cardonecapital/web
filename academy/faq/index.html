Accredited Investor Requirements

Who qualifies and how to verify your status

An accredited investor is an individual or entity that meets specific financial thresholds set by the SEC. For individuals, this means:

  • Income: Annual income exceeding $200,000 (or $300,000 combined with a spouse/domestic partner) in each of the two most recent years, with a reasonable expectation of the same in the current year.
  • Net Worth: A net worth exceeding $1 million, excluding the value of your primary residence.
  • Professional Certification: Holders of Series 7, 65, or 82 licenses in good standing also qualify under SEC rules.

Cardone Capital funds are offered exclusively to accredited investors under Regulation D. This ensures every investor has the financial sophistication and capacity to evaluate and bear the risks of a private placement.

IRA & 401k Investing

Use retirement dollars to invest in real estate — tax-advantaged

Yes — Cardone Capital accepts investments from Self-Directed IRAs (SD-IRAs) and Solo 401k plans. This is one of the most powerful strategies available because you can deploy tax-advantaged retirement dollars into institutional real estate.

To invest with retirement capital, you need a custodian that permits alternative investments — such as Equity Trust, Millennium Trust, or Forge Trust. The process:

  1. Open a self-directed IRA or Solo 401k account with an approved custodian
  2. Roll over or transfer funds from your existing IRA or 401k (tax-free if done correctly)
  3. Direct the custodian to invest on behalf of your retirement account
  4. Receive returns tax-deferred (Traditional) or tax-free (Roth)

Our team has guided hundreds of investors through this process. Text "IRA" to 305-407-0276 or book a call to receive our free IRA guide.

Book a Call — Free IRA Investing Guide →

A Self-Directed IRA (SD-IRA) is a retirement account that allows you to hold alternative assets — including private real estate fund interests — beyond the typical stock, bond, and mutual fund options offered by traditional brokerages like Fidelity or Vanguard.

To set up an SD-IRA:

  1. Choose a self-directed IRA custodian (Equity Trust, Millennium Trust, Forge Trust, or similar)
  2. Complete the account opening documents
  3. Initiate a rollover or direct transfer from your existing IRA — this is tax-free and penalty-free when done correctly
  4. Once funded, direct the custodian to invest in Cardone Capital on behalf of your IRA

The custodian holds the investment and all related paperwork in the name of your IRA. You remain the beneficial owner. All income and gains flow back into the IRA tax-advantaged.

Investing through a Self-Directed Roth IRA is one of the most powerful strategies in the accredited investor's toolkit. With a Roth IRA:

  • Contributions are made with after-tax dollars — you've already paid tax on what goes in
  • All investment growth is completely tax-free
  • Qualified distributions in retirement are tax-free
  • No required minimum distributions (RMDs) during the owner's lifetime

This means every dollar of real estate distributions, appreciation, and — in Fund 29 — Bitcoin gains could compound and be withdrawn in retirement completely tax-free. The earlier you convert and invest, the longer your tax-free compounding window. Our team works closely with investors on Roth conversion strategies.

Fees, Structure & Legal

Full transparency on how the fund is structured and what you pay

All fees are fully disclosed in each fund's Private Placement Memorandum (PPM). Typical Cardone Capital fund fee components include:

  • Asset Management Fee: An annual fee on committed capital covering ongoing operation, management, and reporting of the portfolio.
  • Acquisition Fee: A one-time fee charged at the time each property is acquired, covering deal sourcing and underwriting.
  • Disposition Fee: A fee charged when a property is sold, covering the exit process and sale execution.
  • Promote / Carried Interest: The GP's share of profits above the preferred return threshold — aligning Cardone Capital's incentives with investor outcomes.

Cardone Capital structures fees to align the GP and LP interests. The promote structure means the management team only captures its full economics when investors achieve strong returns first. Full fee detail is in the PPM.

Risk, Liquidity & Downturns

Honest answers about the risks and how Cardone Capital manages them

Cardone Capital funds are structured as long-term, illiquid private real estate investments. The target holding period is disclosed in each fund's PPM. Private real estate syndication investments are not designed for short-term liquidity — this is a foundational characteristic of the asset class.

There is no public exchange or guaranteed secondary market for limited partnership interests. Transfer of interests may be permitted in certain circumstances subject to GP approval and applicable securities laws, but investors should not rely on this. Only commit capital you genuinely do not need access to for the duration of the intended hold.

This illiquidity is precisely why private real estate has historically delivered premium returns versus publicly traded alternatives — the illiquidity premium is real and meaningful over long time horizons.

Tax, Legal & Documents

K-1s, tax advantages, and what international investors need to know

As a limited partner in a Cardone Capital fund, you receive a Schedule K-1 annually, which reports your proportional share of the fund's income, losses, deductions, and credits for inclusion in your personal tax return.

Real estate partnerships frequently generate significant paper losses from depreciation — including accelerated depreciation through cost segregation studies — which can offset passive income. These deductions can meaningfully reduce the effective tax cost of your investment.

K-1s are typically issued by March 31st annually for the prior tax year. Cardone Capital strongly recommends working with a CPA who has experience in real estate partnership taxation to fully optimize the tax benefits of your investment.

Book a Call — Tax Benefits Discussion →

Private real estate syndication offers some of the most compelling tax advantages in the U.S. tax code:

  • Depreciation: Real estate generates annual paper losses via depreciation even when producing positive cash returns — these losses can offset passive income from other investments.
  • Cost segregation: Accelerated depreciation through cost segregation studies can produce substantial first-year deductions that reduce your taxable income significantly.
  • Bonus depreciation: Current-law bonus depreciation provisions may allow 60–80% first-year writedown of qualifying property components.
  • Long-term capital gains: Appreciation at property sale is taxed at favorable long-term capital gains rates (vs. ordinary income).
  • 1031 exchange potential: Fund-level exchanges at sale can defer capital gains taxes into the next investment.

Individual tax outcomes vary significantly. Always consult a CPA experienced in real estate partnership taxation.
