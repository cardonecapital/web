What is a Self-Directed IRA?

Same Tax Benefits.
More Freedom.

A Self-Directed IRA (SD-IRA) is exactly like a traditional or Roth IRA — it follows the same IRS rules, the same contribution limits, and the same tax treatment. The difference is what you can invest in.

A standard IRA at a brokerage firm restricts you to publicly traded stocks, bonds, and mutual funds. A Self-Directed IRA held at a qualifying custodian opens the door to alternative investments — including private real estate funds like Cardone Capital.

The tax advantages are identical: a Traditional SD-IRA grows tax-deferred, and a Roth SD-IRA can grow entirely tax-free. Your money works the same way — it's just working in real estate instead of the stock market.

Custodian Guide

What Is an SD-IRA Custodian & How Do You Choose One?

An SD-IRA custodian is an IRS-approved, regulated financial institution that holds your Self-Directed IRA assets on your behalf. They handle the account administration, tax reporting (Form 1099-R, 5498), and execute investment instructions you direct to them.

Unlike a brokerage IRA, an SD-IRA custodian does not offer investment advice — they simply hold the assets and process transactions you direct. You as the account holder decide where to invest.

What to Look for in a Custodian

01

Experience with private fund investments

Not all custodians handle private equity or real estate fund subscriptions. Choose one with experience processing alternative asset investments.

02

Clear fee structure

SD-IRA custodians charge account maintenance and transaction fees. Review their fee schedule carefully before opening an account.

03

Responsive to investment processing timelines

Slow custodians can delay your investment. Ask about their typical processing time for private fund wire transfers.
